# Zenit | Service Management and Reporting System

Zenit is a service management web application developed for an academic business project in the solar energy sector.

The fictional company provides solar panel cleaning services using a robotic cart. The system was created to support the organization and control of operational data, including customer records, service requests, employee information, pending tasks, and business reports.

This project was developed as a freelance collaboration to help a group of FATEC students turn their business idea into a functional digital system.

---

## Project Purpose

The main goal was to centralize operational information in one system and make it easier to manage service requests, customer data, employees, and business performance.

In addition to building the website, I designed and implemented the database structure from scratch. This ensured that the data collected by the system could be organized, queried, and transformed into useful reports.

---

## Main Features

### Customer Registration

The system allows users to register and manage customer information, including:

* CPF
* Full name
* Address
* Phone number
* Email address

This information supports customer identification and enables service requests to be associated with the correct client.

### Service Request Management

Users can create service requests by searching for a customer using their CPF. Once the customer is identified, the system retrieves the registered address to support the service request process.

The system also allows users to monitor pending service requests through status indicators:

* **Green:** completed services
* **Yellow:** pending services
* **Red:** urgent services

This feature supports the prioritization of operational activities and provides a quick overview of service demand.

### Employee Management

The application includes an employee management area where users can register, edit, and delete employee information.

This helps maintain updated operational records and supports the relationship between employees and service activities.

---

## Reporting and Data Analysis Features

The reporting area was developed to transform operational data into information that can support business monitoring and decision-making.

The system provides reports for:

* **Total revenue:** consolidates the financial value generated by registered services
* **Most requested services:** ranks services based on the number of requests
* **Active customers:** identifies customers with registered service activity
* **Total service requests:** monitors the volume of operational demand

All reports can be exported as CSV files, allowing the data to be used for additional analysis in tools such as Excel, Power BI, Python, or SQL.

---

## Relevance to Data Analysis

Although Zenit is a full-stack web development project, it has a strong connection to data analysis because the system was designed to create, organize, and use operational data.

The project demonstrates how data analysis begins before a dashboard or chart is created. Reliable analysis depends on well-structured data collection, consistent records, and a database designed to support business questions.

Through the reporting module, the system transforms raw operational records into business information that can help answer questions such as:

* How much revenue has the company generated?
* Which services are requested most frequently?
* How many customers are currently active?
* What is the total volume of service requests?
* Which service requests require urgent attention?

The CSV export feature also makes the project scalable for future analysis, enabling the data to be explored in external BI and analytics tools.

## Database Design

The database was created from scratch to support the core business processes of the application.

It was designed to organize relationships between customers, employees, service requests, services, and financial information.

The database structure supports:

* Customer identification through CPF
* Storage of contact and address information
* Registration and tracking of service requests
* Employee management
* Service status monitoring
* Revenue calculation
* Generation of reports and CSV exports

## Key Business Value

Zenit was designed to reduce the fragmentation of operational information.

Instead of managing customers, service requests, employees, and reports through separate records, the system centralizes this information and creates a foundation for more organized operations and data-driven decisions.

The reporting area provides a practical example of how operational systems can support business intelligence by turning daily transactions into measurable indicators.
